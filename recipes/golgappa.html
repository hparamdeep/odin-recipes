Golgappa water

Description

Golgappa, also known as phuchka or panipuri is a deep-fried breaded hollow spherical shell, about 1 inch in diameter, filled with a combination of potatoes, raw onions, chickpeas, and spices. It is a common snack and street food in the Indian subcontinent. It is often flavoured with chilli powder, chaat masala, herbs, and other spices.

Ingredients

  • Hot water
  • Tamarind
  • Mint leaves
  • Cumin seeds
  • Fennel seeds
  • Black cardamom
  • Black pepper
  • Dry mango powder
  • Chaat masala
  • Asafoetida
  • Water
  • Black salt

Steps

  1. Soak the tamarind in hot water, then strain it to collect the smooth pulp. In a blender, mix the mint leaves with a little water until smooth.
  2. Lightly roast the cumin seeds, fennel seeds, black cardamom, and black pepper in a pan. Grind them into a fine powder.
  3. Combine the tamarind pulp, mint paste, and ground spice powder in a large jug. Add the dry mango powder, chaat masala, asafoetida, and black salt.
  4. Pour in cold water to reach your preferred intensity. Stir well and let it rest in the fridge for at least an hour so the flavors develop.
