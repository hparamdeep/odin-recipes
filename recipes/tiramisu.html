Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is an Italian dessert made with coffee-soaked ladyfingers (savoiardi) covered with a cream of egg yolks, sugar, and mascarpone and topped with cocoa powder. The dessert originated in northeastern Italy, and modern versions were popularized in restaurants from the late 1960s onward.

Since then, tiramisu has become one of the most internationally recognised Italian desserts and has inspired many variations in home and professional cooking. The name comes from the Italian tirami su, meaning 'pick me up' or 'cheer me up'.

Ingredients

  • Ladyfingers
  • Mascarpone
  • Coffee
  • Heavy Whipped cream
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Vanilla extract
  • Cocoa powder

Steps

  1. Mix creamy filling. Beat the mascarpone, cream, sugar, and vanilla together until stiff peaks.
  2. Dip lady fingers. Add the espresso and liqueur (if using) to a shallow bowl and dip the lady fingers on both sides
  3. Layer mascarpone. Smooth a layer of the mascarpone/whipped cream mixture on top of the lady fingers.
  4. Repeat. Dust with cocoa powder.
